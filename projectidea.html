AutomobileShare

Overview

Topic

AutomobileShare is the project idea of Honest Green Lion. AutomobileShare is a web-based service that allows car owners to list their vehicles with availability for individuals to hire from a few hours to days or weeks. Individuals can search for car brands, availability and location. This is intended to give individuals an efficient experience. There are countless websites from car rentals to share rides such as Kayak, and Autorentals.com, etc (Perkins 2021), however Honest Green Lion looked at this idea and thought why not allow people to make money off their own vehicles. Users will be able to choose a location to pick up the vehicle, have a timeframe the vehicle is available, and set prices depending on what time of the day it is or what day of the week. AutomobileShare is a different concept to the traditional rental of a car. Members of this service have total control over every aspect of this service.
Members will be able to sign up to this service via a website or mobile app. Members will need to provide personal data such as Name, Date of Birth, licence type, state of Issue, any state, territory or federal convictions (such as drink driving, loss of licence offences) (FTC.gov 2012). This data will then be vetted to confirm the member is suitable for this service at this current time. Users of this service will also need to bear in mind if they are involved in an accident an excess of the members vehicle will need to be paid, this will ensure the members who offer their car to this service aren't out of pocket and users cannot treat this service as a free for all.

Motivation

Could your car make you money? Does your vehicle just sit in the garage? Do you worry about upcoming expenses for your vehicle? What if there was a way you could make your vehicle work for you. AutomobileShare may be the answer to your problems. AutomobileShare is a car sharing platform that will allow you to lease out your vehicle when it is not in use. Think of Airbnb but for vehicles. You will be able to set dates for your vehicle’s availability, set prices, set age limits and even approve or disapprove users from your vehicle. You will be 100% in control.

Landscape

A quick internet search for “Car for hire” you will come across multiple websites that have outdated and complicated user interferences and experiences. For example, carjet.com with its messy interference, asks the users to complete multiple actions in one given time. This site doesn’t have a user-friendly interface and is confusing on where to begin. Our website with its clean, user friendly, and modern look is very clear and transparent on where the user should begin in their search.
AutomobileShare a website where individuals who own vehicles can list them on the website to allow others to hire it for a few hours or days to earn the car owners some extra money. With the current trend in IT where access to all online listings can be found in one destination is what Honest Green Lion is aiming to get.

Roles

There are many roles that need to be assigned in order to get a proper website out there, like website developer, marketing strategist, UX designer, lead developer, lead designer, you name it! (Wahhab 2016 & Diffily 2013). However, while working on our project, Honest Green Lion did not really assign any specific roles. All the members did what they felt most comfortable with doing. Ayesha was assigned the task of designing the website, which she accomplished using Adobe XD. Matthew has worked on presenting our idea to potential employers/ venture capitalists, and the rest of the members worked on the deliverables for A3 and A5. HGL has yet, not found the time to begin working on the development of the actual website, however, we do plan to work on it, so hopefully soon enough, we will have something up and running!

Testing

In order to test our project, we will first interview potential users, even before starting with the project, so that we can have a rough idea about what the users are expecting from us and what their goals are. A quick findings report will be made to summarise all the information found from the interviews and a prototype will be made accordingly (Nielson 2005). Once the prototype is ready, usability testing will be conducted. Usability testing is very important as it saves development money and time, and raises the possibility of success (Praxent 2019). Once the potential users have identified their frustrations, we will re-design the website. This process will be repeated until we come up with the best possible solution. This way, we can make sure that the chances of our website succeeding is high.
